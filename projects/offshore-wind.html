Offshore Wind Risk Analysis

Risk Analysis ¬∑ Weather Window ¬∑ Data Calibration ¬∑ Logistics Optimization

Project Description & Objective

Assessed and quantified construction downtime risks caused by extreme wave conditions to minimize weather-related delays and optimize offshore logistics for turbine installation and foundation work. This project supported an offshore wind constructor in identifying safe operational windows, ensuring that project timelines and vessel deployments were grounded in precise environmental risk data.

Methods & Data

The analysis relied on wave-occurrence frequency modeling to evaluate operational safety. First, I performed risk evaluation using R to analyze historical wave height (Hs) thresholds of 1.2m and 1.5m. To ensure the reliability of the measurements, I integrated historical datasets with on-site buoy measurements through rigorous calibration of private observations.

Finally, I translated complex metocean datasets into actionable insights by highlighting La Ni√±a years to observe the influence of macro-climate patterns on sea state severity. This enabled a high-fidelity risk profile, facilitating effective construction scheduling and proactive project management across various engineering timelines.

Key Deliverables

The heatmaps reveal a clear seasonal window for offshore operations, typically between May and August. Conversely, winter months present significantly higher risks of wave activity.

La Ni√±a Influence: Years marked in red (e.g., 2011, 2017 and 2020) show intensified winter peaks where wave occurrence probabilities exceed 80%. This reinforces the monsoon effect, leading to a more hostile maritime environment and a further restricted operational window compared to neutral years.

The scatter plot indicates that the two measurement sources were not consistently aligned. Due to the high RMSE and low correlation, I advised the client that these sources could not be used interchangeably, highlighting the necessity of maintaining rigorous private sensor maintenance for reliable future forecasting.

Impact & Insights

This analysis successfully established a correlation between macro-climate events and localized sea state severity, providing a robust risk profile for the client's engineering department. By identifying the critical weather window constraints for 1.2m and 1.5m thresholds, the project allowed the constructor to optimize vessel mobilization and mitigate the financial impact of weather-related downtime.
