Project Description & Objective

By monitoring intra-cloud (IC) lightning-jump signals from an advanced detection system, this project leverages meteorological correlations between IC activity and the onset of severe convective rainfall. This integration enhances weather guidance within a 30 km domain, providing a semiconductor company with actionable insights to safeguard product yield and support economically informed decisions under weather-related risks, while simultaneously enabling the client to develop specialized early-warning applications.

Methods & Data

The project utilizes high-resolution IC lightning data sourced via an international meteorological provider. To ensure consistency, the dataset was cross-validated against regional observations from Taiwan Power Company. Based on the client‚Äôs site coordinates, I analyzed lightning-jump signals across multiple spatial scales (5, 10, 20, and 30 km radii). This integrated approach serves as a critical indicator for forecasting extreme convective rainfall events and enhancing automated early warning systems.

Impact & Insights

This project represents the first initiative in Taiwan to utilize intra-cloud lightning-jump signals for predictive severe weather forecasting. By shifting from traditional radar-only methods to advanced convective detection, the model offers a significant lead-time advantage for high-stakes manufacturing environments.

Following its successful implementation in the semiconductor industry to safeguard yield, the model has demonstrated strong cross-industry scalability.
