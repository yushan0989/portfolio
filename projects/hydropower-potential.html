Hydropower Potential Investment

Python ¬∑ Climate Scenario ¬∑ Hindcasting ¬∑ Long-term Climate Forecasts

Project Description & Objective

Developed a strategic assessment for a Tier-1 manufacturing partner within a global consumer electronics supply chain, driven by the parent company's ambitious mandate to achieve carbon neutrality by 2030. The primary objective was to translate complex climate-scenario data into insightful energy yield proxies, enabling the client to make informed decisions regarding capital investment and capacity planning for the next several decades.

Methods & Data

The analysis followed a rigorous four-stage framework to ensure the reliability of long-term energy projections. First, I performed a statistical validation to confirm the high positive correlation between regional precipitation and historical output. After selecting the optimal climate models, I gridded the meteorological data to align with the specific coordinates of the client‚Äôs sites.

Machine Learning & Backtesting: Using a 20-year historical dataset, I employed a split-window validation (hindcasting) technique to train and test multiple machine-learning regression models. The model with the lowest MAE was then selected to simulate yields across four RCP scenarios (2.6, 4.5, 6.0, and 8.5).

This approach allowed for time-scale alignment between historical records and CMIP6 projections, providing climate scenario interpretation that translates raw data into clear risk metrics for strategic planning. This ensured the client‚Äôs renewable energy strategy accounted for climate volatility and long-term investment resilience.

Key Deliverables

Figure 1 demonstrate the initial validation phase. By establishing a strong statistical link between meteorological inputs and energy output, the model achieved a high degree of fit during the historical training period, ensuring the baseline for future projections was robust. Figure 2 highlights a critical technical deliverable: the time-lagged correlation. Through iterative testing of various monthly moving averages, I identified that a three-month moving average (3-MMA) most effectively captures the lagged relationship between rainfall and power generation. Unlike solar energy, hydropower yields are dependent on catchment accumulation time; accounting for this lag improved model accuracy significantly.

Figure 3 demonstrates high predictive accuracy by closely tracking 10 years of historical energy fluctuations and seasonal peaks. This successful hindcasting validates the machine learning framework as a robust tool for simulating future energy yields across various climate scenarios. Figure 4 shows the final model selection process, one model was identified as having the lowest MAE, making it the primary driver for future simulations.

Figure 5 presents the final energy yield proxies across four IPCC Representative Concentration Pathways (RCPs). By presenting the client with a range of outcomes from the low-emission RCP 2.6 to the high-emission RCP 8.5, I provided the client with a way to stress-test their capital investments and ensure their long-term plans remain solid under various future climate conditions.

Impact & Insights

This project was a turning point in my career. Working closely with the client, I realised that while the science of climate change is clear, financial considerations are the primary driver for corporate renewable energy transitions. This insight is exactly what inspired me to move to the Netherlands to specialise in Environmental Economics, as I wanted to understand the market mechanisms that make large-scale sustainability possible.

The experience also sharpened my professional communication. I learned how to translate complex data into a persuasive consulting report that speaks the language of stakeholders. Furthermore, narrowing down the project scope gave me unique energy market insights, such as the strategic reasons why certain enterprises prioritise the stability of hydropower over the variability of solar energy.
