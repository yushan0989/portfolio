Solar Energy Forecasting

R Shiny ¬∑ Machine Learning ¬∑ Renewable Energy

Project Description & Objective

To enable clients to better manage short-term variability in solar generation, this project aimed at improving electricity dispatch, grid efficiency, and maintenance planning. By bridging the gap between raw meteorological data and actionable business decisions, the forecasting system provides energy operators with a reliable tool for real-time operational management.

Methods & Data

I implemented linear regression and machine learning models leveraging historical datasets including cloud cover, solar irradiance, and electricity production records. To make these insights accessible, I developed an interactive R Shiny dashboard that visualizes real-time forecasts alongside scheduled maintenance windows.

Beyond technical development, I led monthly performance calibrations and presented data-driven findings directly to clients. This ensured the models remained aligned with evolving operational goals and allowed for the continuous refinement of maintenance strategies based on predictive accuracy.

Impact & Insights

The forecasting model achieved high stability with a monthly nMAE of <5%. This level of precision provides energy operators with the confidence needed for effective electricity dispatch and preventive maintenance. By successfully translating meteorological variables into grid-efficiency metrics, the project demonstrated how predictive analytics can mitigate the risks associated with renewable energy variability.
